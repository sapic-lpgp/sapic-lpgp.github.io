Plasma physics

Magnetized plasmas are governed by the interplay of particle dynamics and electromagnetic fields. A key quantity is the plasma frequency, which sets the timescale for collective electron oscillations:

Magnetron discharges

In magnetron sputtering, a static magnetic field B\mathbf{B}B is applied close to the target surface to trap electrons via the E×B\mathbf{E} \times \mathbf{B}E×B drift:

vE×B=E×B∣B∣2\mathbf{v}_{E \times B} = \frac{\mathbf{E} \times \mathbf{B}}{|\mathbf{B}|^2}vE×B​=∣B∣2E×B​

This drift is responsible for the azimuthal electron current that sustains the discharge and can give rise to coherent rotating structures known as spokes.
