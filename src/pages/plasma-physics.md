# Plasma physics

Magnetized plasmas are governed by the interplay of particle dynamics and electromagnetic fields.
A key quantity is the plasma frequency, which sets the timescale for collective electron oscillations:

$$
\omega_{pe} = \sqrt{\frac{n_e e^2}{\varepsilon_0 m_e}}
$$

where $n_e$ is the electron number density, $e$ the elementary charge, $\varepsilon_0$ the permittivity of free space, and $m_e$ the electron mass.

## Magnetron discharges

In magnetron sputtering, a static magnetic field $\mathbf{B}$ is applied close to the target surface to trap electrons via the $\mathbf{E} \times \mathbf{B}$ drift:

$$
\mathbf{v}_{E \times B} = \frac{\mathbf{E} \times \mathbf{B}}{|\mathbf{B}|^2}
$$

This drift is responsible for the azimuthal electron current that sustains the discharge and can give rise to coherent rotating structures known as spokes.
